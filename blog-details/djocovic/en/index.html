Djokovic and Deviation

Serbian Novak Djokovic, born on May 22, 1987, is a professional tennis player whose name everyone has heard today. He is one of three tennis players honored by the tennis society as the 'Big Three' with 17 Grand Slams he won. Having dominated statistically significantly in recent years, Novak won all the matches (46-0) which played in the first 10 months of 2020 and lost to Rafael Nadal in the final at Rolland Garros.

Well, have you heard that such a dominant and successful player has had a deviation operation in the past?

As history marks November 2005, Djokovic was in the tennis world as a young talent, potential star. He believed that he was having a breathing problem and if he solved it, he would be one step ahead of his current rivals. He said that with a 45-minute surgery he had in Milan, "with a deviation from the left side of his nose" and he would now breathe more easily.

“Now I play with the top 10, 20, 50 players more often, but when you can't breathe you need more strength, energy and physical preparation. That is much more difficult. " It has been 15 years since this interview, and while the date marks 2020, Novak Djokovic is the current number one professional tennis player on all charts today. Despite his age, the 33-year-old experienced tennis player plays as a favorite in every tournament he attends.

It is a fact that he also said that one of the reasons for his success is deviation, being able to breathe correctly is critical for such a physical sport. We don't know how long we'll see him on the courts, but there's no doubt he'll always make a name for himself in the greatest tennis player debate ever.
