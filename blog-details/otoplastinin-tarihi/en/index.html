History of Otoplasty

Otoplasty is literally known as ear aesthetics and colloquially as prominent ear surgery. Today, commonly it is made due to the aesthetic concerns of parents about the ears of their children, and in rare cases for functional reasons. In this article, we will talk about the history of otoplasty.

It is known that otoplasty was first described and performed by the physician Sushruta in the 5th century BC in Ancient India. Sushruta told his students that aesthetic operations can be performed not only for the ear but also for other limbs such as lips and nose. It was written in Gentleman's Magazine in the 18th century that the first otoplasty was made by Sushruta.

In the 19th century, in his book "Operational Surgery" written by Johann Friedrich Dieffenbach in 1845, he used the expression prominent ear and explained the stages of its correction.

At the beginning of the 20th century, Harold D. Gilles, who was accepted as the father of plastic surgery, explained how to reconstruct the auricle and pioneered the transition to the modern state of the surgery behind the ear with cartilage structures. In 1964, Radford C. Tanzer showed that the cartilages taken from the individual's own body would give healthier results than the method of cartilage extraction from cadavers, which was used much more frequently at that time.

The current version of the booklet on ear operations published by Dieffenbach in the 21st century was published, and 170 different prominent ear, ear deformity and defect operations techniques were explained.

Today, otoplasty is not only for the auricle, but also includes different types of operations such as congenitally closed ears, and reconstruction of damaged ears.
