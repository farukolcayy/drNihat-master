The Importance of Nose Structure in Nose Aesthetics

The nose resembles a pyramid with its distinctive shape. The nose is designed by evaluating this pyramid-shaped structure before aesthetic operations.

First Priority in Nose Aesthetics: Analysis

Two ways can be followed when analyzing the nose. These ways;

  • Anatomically
  • General analysis of the nose

General Analysis of the Nose

  • First, the height of the nose is checked.
  • The proportion of the nose wings with the eyes is checked.
  • The width of the root of the nose is analyzed.
  • The width of the upper part of the nose and the distance of the nose wings are evaluated.
  • It is ultimately decided by the length of the nose.

Symmetric Analysis

  • The primary criterion is that the midline of the nose is straight.
  • The condition of the eyebrow nose tip aesthetic line (continuing with a slight slope from the eyebrows to the nose tip on the right and left sides) should be evaluated.
  • The cartilage at the tip of the nose and the domes to be formed must be symmetrical.
  • The right and left nasal wings should likewise not interfere with symmetry.

Nasal Back Analysis

In deformities in the nose, the back of the nose definitely changes. This line must be straight. It is recommended to start from 2 mm below the nasion in women on the back of the nose, and a little higher in men.

Analysis of Nose Skin

Oil glands increase on the sides and top of the nose. The skin and supporting tissue are examined manually. Imbalances on the nasal bone and cartilage and deformities on the nose should be analyzed.

Anatomical Analysis of the Nose

The root of the nose is analyzed. The root of the nose should not be too close to the cornea. It should be compatible with the glabella region, and the nose tip angle should be analyzed apart from the nasal root.

When Can You Start Sports After Rhinoplasty?

Those who constantly live with sports can also undergo a rhinoplasty operation. "When can I start sports after nasal aesthetics?" The question can vary quite a bit. Returning to sports life varies according to the sport.

Return to Walk

You can return to the walks in your daily routine 4 days after the surgery, it will be beneficial because of the edema reduction feature of the calm walks. It is recommended 6 weeks after the operation as it will speed up your breathing for returning to high-paced walking.

Return to Run

For running, it can be said that it is suitable for 6 weeks after the operation. To increase the tempo, first make sure that you do not feel the pressure in your head. If it's okay, nothing can hold you back.

Return to Swim

For swimming, you can start swimming after 4 weeks after your operation. You should swim carefully and calmly. Trauma may occur in the nose in situations such as jumping.

Return to Fitness, Pilates and Yoga

It is deemed appropriate after 6 weeks for Pilates and yoga, and 3 months later to start sports such as fitness where the whole body is in motion or with high weights. Especially with fitness, it will be beneficial to wait for a while if an increase in pressure and edema on the face is felt.

Return to Football, Basketball and Volleyball

The biggest danger in team sports played with the ball in this style is the ball hit to the nose. Avoiding this is very important. If you are careful, you can return after 4 months. In case of an increase in edema in your nose, it will be useful to wait for a while.

For sports other than those mentioned above, a return can be made after 6 weeks, considering the intensity of body use and the possibility of impact. The critical point for all sports performed after rhinoplasty is to be careful.

What is Deviation? How is it done?

Deviation is the skewness of the nose bone. It is a situation that causes some problems. In this article, we can explain how a healthy nose structure should be before explaining exactly what deviation is.

In a healthy nasal structure, two separate air inlets are also widening towards the back. This expanding section ensures that the incoming air is heated, humidified and sent to the olfactory area. In addition, particles larger than 5 microns taken with air are held and filtered with the help of mucoid secretions on the mucous membrane of the nose. Thus, the air transferred to the lungs becomes clean and of a certain quality. The health of our respiratory and lungs is related to the quality of the air taken.

The area we call the septum in the nose is a cartilage and bone structure that divides the nose into two air inlets, located in the middle of the nostrils and extending to the nasal area. In the deviated nose, this structure is not where it should be. His midline stance may be more or less leaning to the side. Nasal bone skew, which causes problems in the air flow, basically prevents air flow.

Why Does Deviation Occur?

Deviation can occur due to hereditary characteristics or due to asymmetrical face shapes. In addition, deviation may occur as a result of external blows to the nose in the later stages of life, which can usually be in childhood or in adolescence. Nose development continues until the age of 16 in women and 17 in men. Take a blow to the nose during childhood can cause 1 millimeter slippage. If these shifts are not taken into account, the shifts continue to grow as the nose structure develops.

Deviation can cause many problems as it affects a vital situation such as breathing. We can list the problems caused by nasal deviation as follows;

  • With nasal congestion, the person breathes through the mouth. Therefore, the air is not heated and humidified like in the nose, and the quality of the air going to the lung decreases. This causes problems in the lungs and respiratory tract.
  • Because of poor quality air, organs such as throat, larynx and lungs strive to make the air quality. This can cause reactions.
  • Snoring problem occurs.
  • As the breathing quality decreases, the quality of sleep also decreases, and the order is disrupted.
  • Many conditions such as headache and feeling of weakness during the day may be encountered.
  • Excessive weight gain may occur.
  • Diabetes can occur.
  • Heart problems may occur.

The person who cannot breathe through the nose will have to breathe through the mouth. There are some downsides to mouth breathing. We can list these negative aspects for you as follows;

  • Dry mouth.
  • The smell of breath.
  • Problems with teeth and gums.
  • Unrest.
  • Lack of appetite.
  • Sneezing fits.
  • Poor smell.

Cosmetic and Functional Nose Aesthetics

Cosmetic Rhinoplasty

Cosmetic rhinoplasty is an aesthetics that provides correction of the places that are not pleasing to the eyes and cause discomfort to the person. Unwanted parts are corrected with surgical intervention. Cosmetic rhinoplasty is personal.

  • Asymmetrical nose
  • Nose larger or smaller than necessary,
  • Arched or crooked nose,
  • Very large nostrils are the main causes of cosmetic rhinoplasty.

What is Functional Rhinoplasty?

Functional rhinoplasty, on the other hand, is a surgical intervention performed against various breathing problems. In such operations, the primary goal is to optimize the breathing function, and shape corrections can also be made if the patient wishes. If the individual has both deformity and breathing problems, cosmetic rhinoplasty and functional rhinoplasty can be applied at the same time. Although the purpose of the two types is different, the processes are quite similar.

The main reasons for functional rhinoplasty are as follows;

  • Problems caused by severe traumas,
  • Those who have cartilage problems obstructing the respiratory tract,
  • They are rhinoplasty revisions that have been done before but failed.

Nasal Valve stenosis can be explained as the narrow nostrils. A narrow nasal entrance brings with it breathing problems. Nasal Valve, which is a problem that causes functional rhinoplasty, is usually caused by chronic causes, trauma, nasal concha size or incorrect rhinoplasty.

Problems that cause functional rhinoplasty usually occur during sleep time. Lack of rest, snoring, oral problems caused by mouth breathing and nasal congestion are symptoms of nasal valve problem. Risks of Nose Aesthetics Operation

What Are the Risks?

There are always some risks in surgical interventions. The biggest reason for these is the risks arising from anesthesia. With the tests and analyzes performed, you can minimize these risks and have a smooth operation. The point that should not be forgotten is to inform your doctor about your illnesses. Notifying your doctor about all kinds of illnesses without thinking about what might have to do with it allows you to easily avoid the risks.

First of all, the first risk that is feared to be encountered is the scarring of surgery. However, with the developing technology, sewing processes have much less risk of scarring.

There are also some risks related to age. For instance, a possible nose operation in individuals younger than 16 years in women and 17 in men may cause nasal deformities with their development that has not yet been completed. Therefore, we warn individuals under the age of 16 not to have nasal aesthetics unless they are in a very difficult situation.

In addition, as we mentioned in our article, what should be done before the operation, smoking and alcohol should be avoided for at least 6 months and blood thinners such as aspirin should be stopped at least 10 days in advance.

When we pay attention to all these factors, you can eliminate the risks and pass your operation safely. The point we need to make is to be open and honest with the doctor. If there is no genetic disease, we can eliminate the obstacles to the operation.

Inconvenient Diseases for Nose Rhinoplasty?

Heart disease

Today, the developing health sector has made nasal aesthetics possible for some diseases. People with heart disease are the leading ones. With different anesthesia methods, cardiac patients can also have rhinoplasty. You should definitely consult your heart doctor and eliminate the risks with the necessary tests and analyzes.

Diabetes

There is no obstacle for diabetic patients to undergo rhinoplasty operation, but the point to be known is that the healing process of the wounds takes longer than normal in diabetic patients. At the same time, the body is less resistant to the risk of infection. These risks can be eliminated, but you should pay attention to your doctor's instructions and follow the instructions.

Epilepsy

Theoretically, epilepsy has no obstacle to anesthesia or surgical intervention, however, a seizure that may occur during the operation may pose a life-threatening risk since it cannot be predicted when epileptic seizures will occur. Therefore, we recommend that they do not undergo any operation unless it is necessary.

Asthma

In the same way, asthma disease can be life-threatening in case of asymptomatic and unpredictable seizures during the operation, so we do not recommend any surgical intervention, except in mandatory cases.

Age Limitation for Rhinoplasty Surgery

Although the minimum age limit for rhinoplasty operation varies slightly from person to person, we can generally say 16. The main reason for this is that the development is not yet complete. Changes made in a body that is not fully developed can cause the bones to fit incorrectly. In such cases, revision procedures may become obligatory and have negative consequences for both parties. Therefore, we do not recommend the operation under the age of 16 in the absence of severe trauma or psychological effects.

Is Rhinoplasty Performed After a Certain Age?

We talked about the lower limit for rhinoplasty, but is there an upper limit? There is no upper limit determined theoretically, however, after the age of 60, the body starts to lose its power to regenerate itself. As a result of this situation, the scars do not heal at the desired level and it becomes difficult to achieve the desired results. Considering all these, we do not recommend any surgical operation after the age of 60, except for mandatory cases.
