Lip Filler

Lip filler is an aesthetic intervention based on injecting dermal fillers in and around the inner part of the lips to add volume or shape the lip structure. This procedure can be applied by experts to anyone who experiences lip thinning for various reasons or wants to change their lip structure.

How is lip filling done?

Lips are a region where many nerve endings are dense due to its general structure. Therefore, local numbing of the area before the filling process will make the lip filling process more comfortable. The use of good local anesthesia or local anesthesia by injection, as well as taking oral painkillers a while before the process, makes the process easier. Before the lip filling process, it is necessary to talk to the patient in detail and to understand his/her expectations about how she/he wants to see her lips. After a good examination, the filling process is started. With the help of fine-tipped needles or a cannula, the desired amount of filler is injected into the lip as previously planned. Lip filling procedure should be done by experienced hands and by specialist doctors.

For what purposes is lip filling performed? Who can have lip filler application?

  • Anyone can have lip fillers if they want.
  • Clarifying the lip contour.
  • Correction of lip asymmetries.
  • Elimination of fine lines on lips.
  • In reducing lip edge sagging.
  • Gummy smile- lip filling is frequently applied in cases where gums appear while laughing.

Where can lip filling be applied on the lips?

Lip filling is not done to a standard place for everyone. Sometimes it can be done in the form of a frame only on the red area of the lip, sometimes only on the area called lip contour, where the lip and skin meet. As a result, it can be applied to any area of the lower and upper lips to provide an aesthetic appearance by evaluating personal expectations.

What should be considered after lip filler?

Lip filling is a special application. It will be correct to do this process by experienced people. Very rarely, there may be bruising and swelling, in this case it can be easily controlled. This situation recovers in a short time by applying ice and bruising creams to the area. One week to 10 days after the lip filling application, the patient is called for control and, although very rarely, some points can be filled for a second time. Thus, the process ends. Lip filling does not only give the lips a fullness, youthful and fresh look, but also gives youth and dynamism to the whole face.

What are the effects of lip filling?

Immediately after the application, the volume of the lips increases, the lip contour and corners become clear. Since hyaluronic acid is a substance with high water retention, lips appear moister and more pinkish after filling application.

Will there be edema and swelling after lip filling application?

Sometimes there may be swelling, edema and bruising in the first days after the application. But these findings will go away within a few days. Since the consistency of the filler we use is soft, these findings quickly regress.

Is lip filling permanent?

One of the most curious questions of those who have lip filling is whether the lip filling is permanent or not. Since permanent fillers cause irreversible reactions on the skin, we use fillers which last up to a maximum of one year that are softer and do not harm the lips. This is much healthier.

Points to be considered in lip filling

The filler used is very important. Another important issue is that the doctor who will apply should be experienced in this field. It is very significant for the integrity and harmony of the face to put the proper amount of filler on the lips and to shape the lips well. When a lot of filling is applied to the upper lip, a bad appearance occurs in the form of lips that are called 'duck lips' which extending forward. Lip filling application is a very sensitive process. For this reason, good results can be obtained only by an experienced doctor, with the correct proportions and use of the right material.
